Community Involvements

 

Mentor of Vex VRC Rookie Team 81208G

To create opportunities for more girls to become involved in robotics, Leo has voluntarily been the build and programming mentor of a Vex Robotics Competition all-girls team. At the beginning of the 2020-2021 season, Leo started to teach the primary team members on how to build the robot’s base, including different techniques, build styles, and the pros and cons of different drive types. As the girl’s team recruited more members, Leo regularly teaches basic mechanical skills, ranging from chain linking for speed, to how various gear ratios affects the drive chain’s capabilities. Leo also taught how to use different sensors like the encoders and light sensors. Later on, Leo focused on demonstrating how to program the robot with C++ to allow for autonomous driving in a straight line, turning precisely, how to tune the programs, and how to correct autonomous mistakes with the principles of PID control.


 

Mentor of Vex VRC Rookie Team 18068A

During the 2019-2020 season, Leo voluntarily mentored the Vex Robotics Competition rookie team 18068A, in which he mainly coached the junior members on the basic knowledge of robotics. Since the rookie team contains members from various schools including elementary schools, Leo trained the junior members the basic building skills, from identifying different types of tools and materials, to designing the chassis of a reliable robot, etc. As the senior members of the rookie team have basic programming skills, Leo created the chance to let them experience the application of individual sensors on the robot to improve performance and develop programming skills. This included the V5 Camera for object detection on the robot and how to program it so that the robot can detect the targeted object in the field. Additionally, the senior rookie members learned various other sensors, including the use of light sensors on the intake mechanism of the robot used to help improve the robot accuracy in autonomous and drive control. As a result, team 18068A became the Tournament Champions at the Toronto Qualifying event and qualified to compete at the 2020 Vex Ontario Championships.


 

Workshop in Markham Village Library: Robot Demo Show and Coding Class

In order to promote VEX Robotics and STEM learning to more girls, on February 4th 2019 at Markham Village Library, Leo along with his teammates hosted a robot demo show during the program “Coding for girls”. The team members showcased how to use the remote control to drive various VEX robots, including VEX IQ and VEX VRC and Lego EV3 robots, as well as how to start simple coding/programming. The purpose of this event is to promote more girls to involve to robotic and engineering field by showcasing how robotics is related to our daily life and how robotics use engineering method to bring up practical solution.


 

Mentor of the Welcome Centre Workshop

From January 24th to March 21th 2019, with the assistance from one of the sponsor of LeRoi Robotics, STEM in Box, every Thursday night from 5pm-7pm Leo demonstrated how to build a sumo robot by using Lego pieces , and taught various students how to program the robot at the Welcome Centre Markham South Workshop.

The students were all new immigrants to Canada. Their age were betwen 14 to 20 years old, and had no experience with robotics. Through the event, Leo promoted robotics and STEM.

Robots Demo at Coledale Public School on November 22, 2018

As a former student from Coledale Public School, Leo continued to promote STEM learning to his elementary school. To spread VEX robotics learning and to pass on their knowledge and skills to the next generation, Leo along with his team members hosted a robotics demo show at the parent-teacher interview night.

The team members demonstrated how to remotely control different types of robots, including Lego EV3 robots, Vex IQ robots, and Vex VRC robots. All the junior students got to learn how to use the tablet to control a robot, how to create simple programs with the Mindstorm programming environment and how to code using Robot-C. This successful demo brought up youth interest in robotics, and promoted the students to join the schools robotics team.
