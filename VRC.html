    • LeRoi Robotics 81208X

    Leo founded an independent community robotics team, Vex VRC Robotics Competition team 81208X LeRoi Robotics, in order to provide opportunities to youth who wants to become involved with robotics.

    Leo devoted his passion and enthusiasm towards managing and maintaining the team’s daily routines, from robot design and build, programming techniques, creating the engineering notebook, to recruiting new members and arranging outreach events.

    His team members come from various high schools in York region, including St. Robert CHS, Unionville HS, Thorn lea HS, Markville SS, Pierre Elliot Trudeau HS, and Coledale PS. Along with the team, Leo hosted various outreach events aimed at promoting robotics throughout the community, including, but not limited to, a performance at Coledale PS in 2018 and 2019, running workshops at the Markham Public library in 2019, and creating an all-girls team in 2020-2021 season. Team 81208X won many awards while continuously mentoring rookie teams 18068A The Thornhill Knights and 81208G LeRoi Galaxy.

    Below is a list of 81208X’s achievements:

  • 2020-2021 season, Team 81208X won multiple Skills Challenge Champion, ranking 1st place nationally in Canada, and was ranked 1st globally in skills from Feb 13 to Mar 13, currently 3rd globally in skills.
  • Founded all-girls team 81208G, volunteered to mentor them during the COVID-19 pandemic through virtual platforms
  • 2019-2020 season, Team 81208X won the Tournament Champion award, Think Award, and Provincial Create Award that qualified to the 2020 Vex World International Championship Competition
  • Helped 18068A win Tournament Champion at the Toronto Regional in the 2019-2020 season, qualifying for the 2020 Ontario Provincial Championships
  • 2018-2019 season, team won the Excellence Award and Judges Award

    2019-2020 Vex Robotics Competition: Tower Takeover

    Custom gyro mounting on rubber shock links to absorb vibration from the robot and improve sensor readings

  • Line trackers for precise intake motion for cube position
  • Designed custom flap intake system

  • Touch sensor for resetting tilter mechanism
  • Custom tilter gearing using 1:15 reduction for added strength without sacrificing scoring times, allowed for up to 13 cube capacity

    Main tray allowed for up to 13 cubes capacity

    Anti-tip designed at the back to prevent from tipping

    2018-2019 Vex Robotics Competition: Turning Point

    Gyro for heading correction, encoders for autonomous movement, integrated motor controllers and encoders for cap arm positions and automatic piston drawback positions for firing balls

    Designed wide length intake for easy fielding and improved cycle times during ball firing

    Designed custom efficient cap lift for high scoring and low scoring capabilities, functioned as platform ram when needed

  • The flywheel was used to launch balls at the flags at different heights. Using a geared up ratio of 1:25 on 100rpm torque motors, the flywheel was able to hit the flags from all positions on the field.
  • A wide roller intake was used to feed the balls into the robot, and a geared up conveyer system brought the balls into the flywheel.

    The flywheel is located at the front of the bot, the cascade elevator is housed at the back.

    Illustrated is one stage of the cascading elevator. Using the interlocking edges of the channels, each stage was created and tuned individually to fit into the slots.

    Dual chain links were used to drive each stage simultaneously, in the event of a lift failure, the elevator would still function due to its second chain link.

    Designed custom cascading elevator lift, 4 stages with high pole capacity
